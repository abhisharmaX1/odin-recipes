Roti

Description

The chapati is the staple flat bread of Northern India and Pakistan, also known as a roti. It can be made from many types of grain, but is most commonly made with finely ground whole wheat flour.

Ingredients

  • Whole wheat flour
  • Salt to taste
  • Warm water as required

Steps

  1. Knead flour and water together.
  2. Cover for atleast 5 minutes.
  3. Divide into balls and roll out into disks.
  4. Place a non-oiled pan on a moderately high fire.
  5. When very hot, put a chapati on the pan and press it flat.
  6. Flip and repeat so it becomes lightly browned on both sides.
  7. Chapatis are often brushed with ghee (clarified butter) after being cooked.
